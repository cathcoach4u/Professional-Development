## PORT Step Descriptions

**Step 1 — Presence (Pair Share)**
The practitioner establishes attunement and full presence before any intervention begins. Pair share activates co-regulation.

**Step 2 — Observation (Passive Listening)**
Listening without directing. The practitioner notices patterns, tone, and relational dynamics without interrupting the natural flow.

**Step 3 — Identification (Spotting Killers)**
Recognising the communication and relational patterns that damage connection. These are named and tracked without yet intervening.

**Step 4 — Disruption (Down the Middle)**
The practitioner enters the dynamic to interrupt harmful cycles. This is a direct, timed intervention.

**Step 5 — Correction (Introducing Antidotes)**
Antidotes to the identified killers are introduced. New patterns are modelled and practised.

**Step 6 — Mastery (Integration)**
The couple or individual integrates the new patterns independently. The practitioner steps back and tracks consolidation.
