# Relate

Relate is a New Zealand-based relationship therapy organisation that develops and delivers PORT (Presence Oriented Relationship Therapy). Relate provides training, resources, and intensive programmes for practitioners working with couples and individuals.

## Why PORT Was Created

PORT was developed by Steven Dromgool to fill a critical gap in the couples therapy field and to make high-quality couples therapy training scalable and accessible.

**The problems PORT addresses:**

- **Many therapists aren't trained in couples work.** Research shows untrained therapists have only a 50-55% success rate — about the same as doing nothing at all.
- **Access and affordability are barriers.** The time and cost of training new therapists — especially in New Zealand — was high and slow-moving.
- **Training new therapists was inefficient.** It often took over a year to become competent in couples work, leaving many clients underserved during the learning curve.

**The solution PORT provides:**

- **Designed to onboard therapists quickly** by drawing on the best elements of existing couples therapy models (Gottman, EFT, Imago, PACT) and organising them into a cohesive, accelerated learning framework.
- **Bite-sized, topic-specific modules** apply modern learning theory to help therapists gain competence fast.
- **Scalable and affordable**, enabling interns and developing therapists to start offering effective therapy sooner.

---

## What Relate Does

- **PORT** — A structured, evidence-informed approach to couples therapy built around six progressive mastery steps — from Presence through to Integration. Grounded in Gottman, Imago, and attachment theory.
- **Training Programmes** — Level 1 (self-paced) and Level 2 (live practice intensive) PORT training for practitioners.
- **Resources** — A suite of infographics, dialogue scripts, and clinical tools including the 4 Horsemen framework, the Bridge model, Deep Listening, the Anger Shield, and the Cycle of Relationship.
- **Community** — Practitioner Facebook group for participants to connect and continue learning.
